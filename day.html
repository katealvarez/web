Day

The song "Nights," begins with a description of someone from Frank Ocean's past, perhaps a past lover or a past version of himself. This other entity is someone that Frank is trying to escape when he says

If I get my money right

You know I won't need you.

It's also clear that Frank is under extreme stress through the fast-paced beat, continuous lyrics, and repeating of words, as one feels when they're sleep deprived. Ocean's sleep deprivation sparks from working a night shift and taking care of a baby, reflecting stressful periods he has lived in his life that make him relatable to his audience. However, as the song nears its transition, we see Frank give in to short-term relief saying

rolling marijuana, thats a cheap vacation.

At his lowest point, lets in this past entity back into his life when he says

I'll buzz you in, just let me know when you outside.

To summarize, the beginning portion of the song "Nights" describes Frank Ocean ending his stressful day, becoming more and more miserable as the day drags out into night.
