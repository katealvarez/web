Night

The second part of "Nights" is much more raw and slow-paced than the first, reflective of the feeling of serenity and calm in the middle of the night. The song represents the transition from a hectic day and swtiched to a tranquil, comforting night, which Frank is now taking advantage of. In this part, Frank also takes the time to reminisce in the past, specifically the past entitity he tried to escape. This is evident when he says

After 'Trina hit I had to transfer campus

Your apartment out in Houston's where I waited

Stayin' with you when I didn't have a address.

"Trina" refers to Hurricane Katrina, which left Frank homeless and forced to stay with the antagonist of this song. Frank takes this time to reminisce on simpler But the nostalgia is short-lived, consisting of less than half as many lines as the metaphorical day. Sadly, Frank's reminiscence is quickly overtaken by thoughts of his next work shift. Thus the song "Nights" is only a fraction of the endless cycle of working, burnout, quick satisfaction, and working again, a reality for many people.
